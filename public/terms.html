Finance Terms 101

Debit card

  • A plastic magnetic card that you use to access your money in your bank an make purchases.

PIN

  • It's like a password to your debit card. You enter it when you make a purchase or access your accounts at the bank or ATM. It's usually between 4 to 6 digits. Memorize it and don't share it with anyone. It's a secret!

Chequing Account

  • An account where you can store your money in and use to make purchases.

Savings Account

  • It's similar to a chequing account, but its main purpose is not to pay for purchases; it is to help your money grow with a savings interest rate. Keep on reading to find out what an interest rate is.

Balance and Minimum Balance

  • How much money you have currently in your account. Sometimes an account requires you to keep a minimum balance, for example, a minimum balance of $50; then you cannot have money less $50 in the account.

Deposit

  • Putting money IN your bank accounts. The opposite of withdraw. You can tell your bank teller 'Hi, I would like to deposit some cash'

Withdraw

  • Taking out money (cash) OUT of your bank account. The opposite of deposit. You can tell your bank teller 'Hi, I would like to withdraw some money.'

Transfer

  • Transferring money between your accounts. You can tell your bank teller 'Hi, I would like to transfer some money from my chequing to my savings.'

Trasaction

  • An action you can do with your bank accounts; for example, deposit, withdraw and transfer.

Monthly Transaction Limit

  • The number of transactions (remember, deposit, withdraw and transfer)you can have per month. Don't go over this number!

Savings Interest Rate

  • It's a feature of the saving account. If the interest rate is 2%, you get 100% + 2% = 102% of your money in your savings account.

Automated Teller Machine (ATM)

  • A machine that allows you to deposit and withdraw money from your accounts. There is a limit on how much you can take out. Don't go over this limit!
